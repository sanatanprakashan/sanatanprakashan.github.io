Our Story

In 1965, Sri Ballabh Press was founded. Then in 1983, Sanatan Prakashan Mandir was formally established as the publishing division of Sri Ballabh Press, located on Mathura Road, Hathras, Uttar Pradesh. Founded by Shri Krishna Ballabh Deen, son of Shri Radha Ballabh and father of Shri Mangal Pandey, the organization was created with a singular purpose—to publish and disseminate books with authenticity, quality, and lasting value, supported by the long-standing capabilities of Sri Ballabh Press.

The surviving documents trace this journey—from the original application submitted through Patents & Trade Marks (India) Regd., Aligarh, to the legal declaration executed before the Sub-Registrar, Sealdah (24 Parganas).

The legal declaration further affirmed that “Sanatan Prakashan Mandir” was the exclusive trade name of the proprietor and had been adopted for use across books, printed publications, papers, documents, containers, stationery, embossing, stamps, and other materials associated with the publishing business. The declaration asserted sole ownership of the trade name, affirmed the exclusive right to use it throughout India, and stated that any unauthorized use, imitation, or counterfeit use would constitute an infringement of those rights and be subject to legal action under the applicable laws.

Over the years, Sanatan Prakashan Mandir has published a diverse range of religious and literary works. Among its notable publications are Shri Krishna Chalisa and Ganesh Chalisa, along with numerous other devotional booklets and publications. The publishing house also brought out the daily newspaper Dainik Krantinaad, and from 1993 onwards published the newspaper Adi Sanatan, reflecting its integrated role as both a publisher and a printer serving readers with quality printed literature.
